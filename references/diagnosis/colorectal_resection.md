# Colorectal Cancer Resection - Required Reporting Elements

## Overview
This reference covers reporting elements for primary colorectal carcinoma (resection specimens) from both CAP and ICCR guidelines. ICCR dataset includes 18 core and 7 non-core elements.

## Element Classification
- **CORE/REQUIRED**: Must be present in every report
- **CONDITIONAL**: Required when applicable (specimen/site-specific)
- **RECOMMENDED/NON-CORE**: Encouraged but not mandatory

---

## CORE ELEMENTS (Both CAP & ICCR)

### 1. Specimen Type / Procedure
- Right hemicolectomy
- Extended right hemicolectomy
- Transverse colectomy
- Left hemicolectomy
- Sigmoid colectomy
- Low anterior resection
- Abdominoperineal resection (APR)
- Total colectomy
- Total proctocolectomy
- Segmental resection
- Local excision (separate dataset)
- Other (specify)

### 2. Tumor Site
- Cecum
- Ascending colon
- Hepatic flexure
- Transverse colon
- Splenic flexure
- Descending colon
- Sigmoid colon
- Rectosigmoid junction
- Rectum (upper, mid, lower)
- Overlapping / cannot be determined

### 3. Tumor Size
- Greatest dimension in mm
- Additional dimensions
- Circumferential involvement (percentage or description)

### 4. Macroscopic Tumor Perforation
- Not identified
- Present (through tumor)
- Present (peritoneal surface away from tumor)
- Indeterminate

### 5. Histologic Type
WHO Classification:
- Adenocarcinoma NOS
- Mucinous adenocarcinoma (>50% mucinous)
- Signet-ring cell carcinoma (>50% signet-ring cells)
- Medullary carcinoma
- Adenosquamous carcinoma
- Squamous cell carcinoma (rare)
- Undifferentiated carcinoma
- Neuroendocrine carcinoma (high-grade)
- Mixed neuroendocrine-non-neuroendocrine neoplasm (MiNEN)
- Serrated adenocarcinoma
- Micropapillary adenocarcinoma
- Other (specify)

### 6. Histologic Grade (Differentiation)
Four-tier system (AJCC 8th):
- G1: Well differentiated (>95% gland forming)
- G2: Moderately differentiated (50-95% gland forming)
- G3: Poorly differentiated (<50% gland forming)
- G4: Undifferentiated
- GX: Cannot be assessed

Two-tier alternative:
- Low grade (G1, G2)
- High grade (G3, G4, signet-ring, undifferentiated)

### 7. Depth of Invasion / pT Category
- pTis: Carcinoma in situ / intramucosal
- pT1: Submucosa
- pT2: Muscularis propria
- pT3: Through muscularis propria into subserosa/pericolic tissue
- pT4a: Penetrates visceral peritoneum
- pT4b: Directly invades adjacent organs/structures

### 8. Margins
**Proximal margin:**
- Uninvolved by invasive carcinoma
- Involved
- Distance if close (mm)

**Distal margin:**
- Uninvolved by invasive carcinoma
- Involved
- Distance if close (mm)

**Circumferential (radial/mesenteric) margin:**
- Uninvolved (specify distance in mm)
- Involved (tumor at margin)
- Not applicable (segments entirely peritonealized)

ICCR defines positive CRM as tumor ≤1mm from margin

### 9. Lymphovascular Invasion
- Not identified
- Present, intramural only
- Present, extramural
- Present, location not specified
- Cannot be determined

**Extramural venous invasion (EMVI)** - specific reporting encouraged (ICCR core)

### 10. Perineural Invasion
- Not identified
- Present, intramural
- Present, extramural
- Present, location not specified

### 11. Regional Lymph Nodes
- Total number examined (minimum 12 recommended)
- Number with metastatic carcinoma
- Size of largest metastatic deposit
- Presence of extranodal tumor extension
- Tumor deposits (satellite nodules without lymph node structure)

### 12. pN Category
- pN0: No regional lymph node metastasis
- pN1a: 1 positive node
- pN1b: 2-3 positive nodes
- pN1c: Tumor deposits only, no positive nodes
- pN2a: 4-6 positive nodes
- pN2b: 7 or more positive nodes

### 13. Distant Metastasis (if submitted)
- pM1a: One organ/site (specify)
- pM1b: More than one organ/site
- pM1c: Peritoneal metastasis

### 14. Pathologic Stage (pTNM)
AJCC 8th Edition / UICC TNM8 required

## CONDITIONAL ELEMENTS

### Mesorectal Integrity (Rectal specimens only)
- Complete: Intact mesorectum, smooth surface
- Nearly complete: Minor irregularities
- Incomplete: Defects down to muscularis propria

### Mesocolic Plane Assessment (Colon specimens)
- Mesocolic plane: Intact, smooth
- Intramesocolic plane: Irregular, tears
- Muscularis propria plane: Exposed muscle

### Peritoneal Involvement
- Serosal involvement (pT4a)
- Tumor cells on serosal surface
- Inflammatory/mesothelial reaction at serosa

### Treatment Effect (post-neoadjuvant, rectal)
Response grading systems:
- **Modified Ryan/CAP**: 
  - 0: Complete response (no viable cancer)
  - 1: Near complete (single cells or small groups)
  - 2: Partial response (residual cancer with fibrosis)
  - 3: Poor/no response (minimal regression)
- **Dworak/Rödel system**
- **AJCC Tumor Regression Grade (TRG)**

### Relationship to Peritoneal Reflection (Rectal)
- Entirely above
- Astride
- Entirely below

## RECOMMENDED/NON-CORE ELEMENTS

### Tumor Budding
Assessed at invasive front (hotspot method):
- Low: 0-4 buds per 0.785 mm²
- Intermediate: 5-9 buds
- High: ≥10 buds
Report method/normalization factor used

### Tumor Border Configuration
- Infiltrating
- Pushing/expanding

### Microsatellite Instability (MSI) Features
Histologic features suggestive of MSI-H:
- Tumor-infiltrating lymphocytes
- Crohn-like lymphoid reaction
- Mucinous/signet-ring differentiation
- Medullary features
- Poor differentiation
- Heterogeneous morphology

### Mismatch Repair (MMR) Protein Expression
IHC for: MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2
- Intact nuclear expression
- Loss of expression (specify which)
- Requires MLH1 promoter methylation if MLH1/PMS2 loss

### Additional Findings
- Polyp-associated (adenoma, serrated lesion)
- Associated with inflammatory bowel disease
- Synchronous tumors
