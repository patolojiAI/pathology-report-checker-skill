# Exocrine Pancreas Carcinoma - Required Reporting Elements

## Overview
This reference covers reporting elements for carcinoma of the exocrine pancreas (resection specimens) from both CAP and ICCR guidelines. Applies to ductal adenocarcinoma and acinar cell carcinoma. Excludes ampulla of Vater, distal bile duct, duodenal carcinomas, neuroendocrine tumors, and metastatic tumors.

## Element Classification
- **CORE/REQUIRED**: Must be present in every report
- **CONDITIONAL**: Required when applicable (procedure-specific)
- **RECOMMENDED/NON-CORE**: Encouraged but not mandatory

---

## CORE ELEMENTS (Both CAP & ICCR)

### 1. Specimen Type / Procedure
- Pancreaticoduodenectomy (Whipple)
- Pylorus-preserving pancreaticoduodenectomy
- Distal pancreatectomy
- Total pancreatectomy
- Central pancreatectomy
- Enucleation
- Other (specify)

### 2. Tumor Site / Location
Anatomic subdivisions:
- **Head**: Right of the left border of superior mesenteric vein
- **Body**: Between left border of SMV and left border of aorta
- **Tail**: Between left border of aorta and splenic hilum
- Overlapping / Cannot be determined
- Uncinate process (part of head)

### 3. Tumor Focality
- Unifocal (single tumor)
- Multifocal (specify number)

### 4. Tumor Size
- Greatest dimension in mm
- Additional dimensions
- Microscopic size if different from gross

### 5. Histologic Type
WHO Classification of Exocrine Pancreas Tumors:
**Malignant:**
- Ductal adenocarcinoma NOS
- Adenosquamous carcinoma
- Colloid carcinoma (mucinous non-cystic)
- Hepatoid carcinoma
- Medullary carcinoma
- Signet-ring cell carcinoma
- Undifferentiated carcinoma
- Undifferentiated carcinoma with osteoclast-like giant cells
- Acinar cell carcinoma
- Acinar cell cystadenocarcinoma
- Mixed acinar-ductal carcinoma
- Mixed acinar-neuroendocrine carcinoma
- Mixed acinar-neuroendocrine-ductal carcinoma
- Pancreatoblastoma

**Borderline/Uncertain malignant potential:**
- Intraductal papillary mucinous neoplasm with associated invasive carcinoma
- Mucinous cystic neoplasm with associated invasive carcinoma
- Solid pseudopapillary neoplasm

### 6. Histologic Grade (Differentiation)
For adenocarcinomas - based on glandular differentiation:
- G1: Well differentiated (>95% gland forming)
- G2: Moderately differentiated (50-95% gland forming)
- G3: Poorly differentiated (<50% gland forming)
- GX: Cannot be assessed

Note: Signet-ring cell, undifferentiated carcinomas = high grade by definition. Acinar cell carcinomas not typically graded.

### 7. Extent of Invasion
- Confined to pancreas
- Extension beyond pancreas (specify structures):
  - Peripancreatic soft tissue
  - Common bile duct
  - Duodenum
  - Ampulla of Vater
  - Stomach
  - Spleen
  - Colon
  - Adrenal gland
  - Kidney
  - Major vessels (celiac axis, superior mesenteric artery, common hepatic artery)
  - Portal vein / superior mesenteric vein
  - Other (specify)

### 8. Margins
**For Pancreaticoduodenectomy (Whipple):**
- **Pancreatic neck/transection margin**: Distance in mm, or involved
- **Common bile duct margin**: Distance in mm, or involved
- **Proximal (gastric/duodenal) margin**: Uninvolved/involved
- **Distal (duodenal/jejunal) margin**: Uninvolved/involved
- **Uncinate process/retroperitoneal margin (SMA margin)**: Distance in mm
- **Posterior/retroperitoneal margin**: Distance in mm
- **Anterior surface**: Involved/uninvolved (surface, not true margin)
- **Portal vein/SMV groove**: Distance in mm

**For Distal Pancreatectomy:**
- **Pancreatic transection margin**: Distance in mm, or involved
- **Posterior/retroperitoneal margin**: Distance in mm
- **Anterior surface**: Involved/uninvolved

**Margin status definitions:**
- R0: No tumor at margin (distance should be specified)
- R1: Microscopic tumor at margin (≤1mm per ICCR/European consensus)
- R2: Macroscopic residual tumor

### 9. Lymphovascular Invasion
- Not identified
- Present
- Cannot be determined
- Specify: small vessel (lymphatic/venous) vs large vessel

### 10. Perineural Invasion
- Not identified
- Present
- Extent: intrapancreatic vs extrapancreatic

### 11. Regional Lymph Nodes
**For head tumors:**
- Pyloric, anterior/posterior pancreaticoduodenal, common bile duct, proximal mesenteric, hepatic artery

**For body/tail tumors:**
- Splenic hilum, splenic artery, celiac axis, superior/inferior body nodes

Reporting:
- Total number examined (minimum 12 recommended for Whipple)
- Number with metastatic carcinoma
- Size of largest metastatic deposit
- Extranodal extension (present/absent)

### 12. pN Category
AJCC 8th Edition:
- pN0: No regional lymph node metastasis
- pN1: 1-3 positive regional lymph nodes
- pN2: ≥4 positive regional lymph nodes

### 13. pT Category
AJCC 8th Edition:
- pTis: Carcinoma in situ / high-grade PanIN
- pT1: Tumor ≤2 cm
  - pT1a: ≤0.5 cm
  - pT1b: >0.5 cm and <1 cm
  - pT1c: 1-2 cm
- pT2: Tumor >2 cm and ≤4 cm
- pT3: Tumor >4 cm
- pT4: Tumor involves celiac axis, SMA, and/or common hepatic artery

### 14. Pathologic Stage (pTNM)
AJCC 8th Edition / UICC TNM8 required

## CONDITIONAL ELEMENTS

### Associated Precursor Lesions (if present)
- **PanIN** (Pancreatic Intraepithelial Neoplasia):
  - Low-grade PanIN
  - High-grade PanIN
- **IPMN** (Intraductal Papillary Mucinous Neoplasm):
  - Grade of dysplasia
  - Subtype (gastric, intestinal, pancreatobiliary, oncocytic)
  - Main duct vs branch duct
- **MCN** (Mucinous Cystic Neoplasm):
  - Grade of dysplasia

### Treatment Effect (post-neoadjuvant)
CAP/MD Anderson grading:
- **0 (Complete response)**: No viable residual tumor
- **1 (Near complete)**: Single cells or rare small groups
- **2 (Partial response)**: Residual cancer with evident tumor regression
- **3 (Poor/No response)**: Extensive residual cancer, minimal/no regression

Alternative: Evans system (based on % tumor cell destruction)

### Distant Metastasis (if submitted)
- pM1: Distant metastasis present
- Site(s) of metastasis

---

## RECOMMENDED/NON-CORE ELEMENTS

### Tumor Budding
- Assessment at invasive front
- Grading if performed

### Growth Pattern
- Infiltrating
- Expanding/pushing

### Associated Chronic Pancreatitis
- Present
- Absent

### Intrapancreatic Nerve Invasion
- Separate documentation from perineural invasion

### Large Vessel Invasion
- Portal vein invasion
- Superior mesenteric vein invasion
- Arterial invasion

### Additional Findings
- Pancreatic atrophy
- Obstruction of pancreatic/bile duct
- Calcifications

### Biomarkers (if performed)
- KRAS mutation status
- DPC4/SMAD4 IHC
- p53 IHC
- Mismatch repair (MMR) proteins

## MARGIN ASSESSMENT NOTES

The ICCR and European consensus define R1 as tumor within 1mm of margin. CAP traditionally uses tumor at inked margin = positive. Reports should specify:
1. Whether tumor is at margin (ink on tumor)
2. Distance to closest margin if not at margin
3. Which margin is closest/involved

Critical margins for local recurrence:
- Uncinate process/SMA margin (most important)
- Posterior margin
- Portal vein groove
