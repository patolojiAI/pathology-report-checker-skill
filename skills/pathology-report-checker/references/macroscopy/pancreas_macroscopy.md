# Pancreas Specimen Macroscopy Guidelines

Source: AAPA Macroscopic Examination Guidelines (2017), CAP Exocrine Pancreas Protocols
Based on: AJCC/UICC TNM 7th/8th Edition, CAP Protocol 3.4.0.0

---

## Scope

Applies to epithelial tumors of the exocrine pancreas.

**Excluded:**
- Pancreas involvement by tumor from other primary sites
- Tumors of the endocrine pancreas (well-differentiated neuroendocrine tumors)
- Tumors of the Ampulla of Vater

---

## Procedures Covered

| Procedure | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Partial pancreatectomy** | Partial/subtotal or distal resection |
| **Pancreaticoduodenectomy** | Whipple resection |
| **Pylorus-preserving pancreaticoduodenectomy** | Whipple variant preserving pylorus |
| **Total pancreatectomy** | Complete pancreas removal |

---

## Terminology Clarification

### Superior Mesenteric Artery (SMA) Margin

Multiple terms used interchangeably:
- **Uncinate process margin**
- **Retroperitoneal margin**
- **Nonperitonealized surface of uncinate process**
- **Superior mesenteric artery margin** (AJCC preferred)

**Definition:** Soft tissue containing perineural tissue adjacent to right lateral wall of superior mesenteric artery

**Note:** "Retroperitoneal" is potentially confusing since entire pancreas is retroperitoneal

### Posterior Pancreatic Margin

- **CAP:** "Posterior retroperitoneal surface (deep radial surface)"
- **AJCC:** "Posterior pancreatic margin" (soft tissue between IVC anterior surface and posterior pancreatic head)
- **AJCC specifically notes:** This is NOT the retroperitoneal margin

## Molecular Considerations

### miRNA Testing

| Marker | Significance |
|--------|--------------|
| **miR-196a** | Elevated in PDAC; predictive of poor survival |
| **miR-217** | Distinguishes PDAC from non-malignant tissue |

### Immunohistochemistry Panel

| Marker | Pattern in PDAC |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Smad4** | Loss of expression |
| **p53** | Strong nuclear expression |
| **S100P** | Positive (not entirely specific) |
| **Maspin** | Positive (not entirely specific) |
| **IMP-3** | Positive (not entirely specific) |
| **pVHL** | Negative (loss of expression) |

### Tissue Requirements

- Formalin-fixed, paraffin-embedded tissue
- Document fixative used (formalin preferred)
- Document duration of fixation

## Specimen Integrity

### Assessment Requirements

- [ ] Overall specimen integrity
- [ ] Integrity of attached structures
- [ ] Defects or disruptions identified

### Defect Documentation

Include:
1. Anatomic location of defects/disruptions
2. Relationship to tumor
3. Relationship to surgical margins
4. Relationship to circumferential surfaces

**If defects hinder margin assessment:**
- Consult operative report
- Consult surgeon
- Differentially ink and describe defects

## Tumor Documentation

### Tumor Size

- [ ] Three dimensions for all tumors (cm)
- Size differentiates T1 (≤2 cm) vs. T2 (>2 cm) for tumors confined to pancreas
- Tumor size has independent prognostic significance

### Size Assessment Challenges

- Discontinuous growth pattern in some tumors
- Borders may be obscured by:
  - Fibrosis
  - Inflammatory changes
  - Obstructive pancreatitis

**Recommendation:** Thorough and extensive sampling of tumor and margins

### Tumor Site

| Site | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Pancreatic head** | Right of left border of superior mesenteric vein (includes uncinate) |
| **Pancreatic body** | Between left border of SMV and left border of aorta |
| **Pancreatic tail** | Between left border of aorta and splenic hilum |

### Tumor Description

Document:
- [ ] Specific location
- [ ] Relationship to margins
- [ ] Relationship to pancreatic ducts

### Cystic Lesions

Special documentation:
- [ ] Location
- [ ] Characteristics
- [ ] Relationship to pancreatic duct
- [ ] Relationship to bile duct
- [ ] Relationship to other tumors
- [ ] Cyst contents
- [ ] Presence/absence of central scar
- [ ] Communication with pancreatic duct

**Touch preparations:** Valuable for solid pseudopapillary neoplasms

## Depth of Invasion

### Prognostic Factors (Most to Least Significant)

1. **Extent of invasion** (most significant)
2. Regional lymph node metastasis
3. Poorly differentiated histology
4. Increased tumor size
5. Perineural invasion
6. Lymphovascular invasion
7. Incomplete resection

### pT Classification (AJCC 7th/8th Edition)

| Category | Definition |
|----------|------------|
| **pTis** | Carcinoma in situ (includes PanIN-3) |
| **pT1** | Tumor limited to pancreas, ≤2 cm |
| **pT2** | Tumor limited to pancreas, >2 cm |
| **pT3** | Tumor extends beyond pancreas (no celiac axis/SMA involvement) |
| **pT4** | Tumor involves celiac axis or superior mesenteric artery |

### pT3 Extension Includes

- Retroperitoneal fat/soft tissue
- Mesentery
- Greater omentum
- Lesser omentum
- Peritoneum
- Common bile duct
- Duodenum (including ampulla of Vater)

### pT4 Notes

- Considered **unresectable**
- Rarely encountered in surgical pathology
- Portal vein invasion = adverse prognostic factor

### PanIN Lesions

- **PanIN-3** = equivalent to carcinoma in situ (pTis)
- May not be evident macroscopically
- **Entire submission of pancreatic margins warranted** even when grossly free of tumor
- PanIN at resection margin should be mentioned in report

| Category | Definition |
|----------|------------|
| **RX** | Presence of residual tumor cannot be assessed |
| **R0** | No residual tumor |

## Margins

### General Requirements

- [ ] Apply ink to all resection margins
- [ ] State distance of tumor to closest margin (mm or cm)
- [ ] Note if margins grossly involved; specify which margin

### Pancreaticoduodenectomy (Whipple) Margins

| Margin | Sampling Method |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Uncinate process (SMA margin)** | Sections through tumor at closest approach |
| **Posterior pancreatic margin** | Sections through tumor at closest approach + retroperitoneal margin |
| **Common bile duct margin** | Complete en face section |
| **Proximal margin** (gastric/duodenum) | En face or perpendicular sections |
| **Distal margin** (distal duodenal) | En face or perpendicular sections |
| **Pancreatic resection margin** | Complete en face section (includes pancreatic duct) |
| **Anterior margin*** | Sections at closest approach |

*Anterior pancreatic surface is NOT a true resection margin, but involvement correlates with local recurrence

### Total Pancreatectomy Margins

Same as Whipple **without** pancreatic margin

### Partial Pancreatectomy (Distal) Margins

| Margin | Sampling Method |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Proximal pancreatic margin** | Complete en face OR perpendicular sections (depending on tumor location) |
| **Posterior peripancreatic** | Perpendicular sections at closest approach |
| **Anterior peripancreatic** | Perpendicular sections at closest approach |
| **Superior peripancreatic** | Perpendicular sections at closest approach |
| **Inferior peripancreatic** | Perpendicular sections at closest approach |

## Lymph Node Evaluation

### pN Classification (AJCC 7th/8th Edition)

| Category | Definition |
|----------|------------|
| **pNX** | Cannot be assessed |
| **pN0** | No regional lymph node metastasis |
| **pN1** | Regional lymph node metastasis (1-3 nodes, 8th ed) |
| **pN2** | ≥4 regional lymph node metastases (8th ed) |

### Minimum Lymph Node Count

| Source | Minimum Nodes |
|--------|---------------|
| AJCC | 12 regional nodes |
| CAP | 15 nodes (node-negative cases) |

- Lymph node metastasis = independent adverse prognostic factor

### Lymph Node Submission

| Node Type | Submission |
|-----------|------------|
| **Small nodes** | In toto |
| **Large, macroscopically negative** | Serial section, submit entirely |
| **Macroscopically positive** | Representative sampling sufficient |

**Ensure accurate count is possible**

### Technique Note

Serial sectioning in axial plane **perpendicular to duodenum** may produce higher lymph node yield

### Regional Lymph Node Locations

Specific anatomic location not required unless identified or separately submitted by surgeon.

#### Primary Node Groups

| Location | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Superior** | Superior to head and body |
| **Inferior** | Inferior to head and body |
| **Anterior** | Anterior pancreaticoduodenal, pyloric, proximal mesenteric |
| **Posterior** | Posterior pancreaticoduodenal, pericholedochal (CBD), proximal mesenteric |
| **Splenic** | Splenic hilum, tail of pancreas (body/tail tumors only) |

#### Additional Regional Nodes

| Node Group | Applicable To |
|------------|---------------|
| Hepatic artery | All tumors |
| Infrapyloric | Head tumors only |
| Subpyloric | Head tumors only |
| Celiac | Head tumors only |
| Superior mesenteric | All tumors |
| Pancreaticolieno | Body/tail only |
| Splenic | Body/tail only |
| Retroperitoneal | All tumors |
| Lateral aortic | All tumors |

## Distant Metastasis (pM)

| Category | Definition |
|----------|------------|
| **M0** | No distant metastasis |
| **M1** | Distant metastasis |

### M1 Includes

- Positive non-regional lymph nodes
- Peritoneal seeding (even if limited to lesser sac)
- Positive peritoneal cytology (with or without ascites)

### Common Metastatic Sites

1. Liver
2. Peritoneal cavity
3. Lungs

## QA Checklist

### Pre-Analytic
- [ ] Fixative documented (formalin)
- [ ] Fixation duration documented
- [ ] Specimen integrity assessed
- [ ] Defects documented and inked

### Specimen Components
- [ ] All components measured (pancreas, duodenum, stomach, vessels)
- [ ] Soft tissue quantified
- [ ] Additional structures documented

### Tumor Assessment
- [ ] Three dimensions documented
- [ ] Site specified (head/uncinate/body/tail)
- [ ] Relationship to ducts documented
- [ ] Depth of invasion assessed
- [ ] Extension beyond pancreas documented
- [ ] Cystic features documented (if present)

### Margins
- [ ] All margins inked
- [ ] Distance to closest margin documented
- [ ] Uncinate (SMA) margin sampled
- [ ] Posterior margin sampled
- [ ] CBD margin sampled (en face)
- [ ] Pancreatic margin sampled (en face)
- [ ] Proximal/distal GI margins sampled
- [ ] Anterior surface sampled

### Lymph Nodes
- [ ] Total count documented
- [ ] Positive count documented
- [ ] Minimum 12-15 nodes attempted
- [ ] Regional vs. non-regional distinguished

### Additional Elements
- [ ] Perineural invasion assessed
- [ ] Lymphovascular invasion assessed
- [ ] PanIN documented (if present)
- [ ] Treatment effect documented (if applicable)
- [ ] Clinical history documented
