# Breast Invasive Carcinoma - Required Reporting Elements

## Overview
This reference covers reporting elements for invasive carcinoma of the breast (resection specimens) from both CAP and ICCR guidelines.

## Element Classification
- **CORE/REQUIRED**: Must be present in every report
- **CONDITIONAL**: Required when applicable (specimen-specific)
- **RECOMMENDED/NON-CORE**: Encouraged but not mandatory

---

## CORE ELEMENTS (Both CAP & ICCR)

### 1. Specimen Type / Procedure
- Excisional biopsy / lumpectomy
- Re-excision
- Total mastectomy (simple)
- Modified radical mastectomy
- Radical mastectomy
- Other (specify)

### 2. Tumor Site / Laterality
- Right / Left breast
- Clock position (optional)
- Quadrant (upper outer, upper inner, lower outer, lower inner, central)
- Distance from nipple (if applicable)

### 3. Tumor Size (Invasive Component)
- Greatest dimension in mm
- Additional dimensions (optional)
- Method of measurement (gross vs microscopic)
- For multifocal: size of largest focus

### 4. Histologic Type
WHO Classification required:
- Invasive carcinoma of no special type (NST) / Ductal NOS
- Invasive lobular carcinoma
- Tubular carcinoma
- Cribriform carcinoma
- Mucinous carcinoma
- Carcinoma with medullary features
- Invasive micropapillary carcinoma
- Metaplastic carcinoma (specify subtype)
- Mixed carcinoma (specify components)
- Other special types

### 5. Histologic Grade (Nottingham)
Three components scored 1-3:
- **Tubule formation**: 1 (>75%), 2 (10-75%), 3 (<10%)
- **Nuclear pleomorphism**: 1 (small uniform), 2 (moderate variation), 3 (marked variation)
- **Mitotic count**: Score based on field diameter (must specify field area)

Total score → Grade:
- 3-5 = Grade 1 (well differentiated)
- 6-7 = Grade 2 (moderately differentiated)
- 8-9 = Grade 3 (poorly differentiated)

### 6. Margins
- **Status**: Negative (uninvolved), Positive (involved), Cannot be assessed
- **Distance to closest margin**: in mm
- **Location of closest margin**: Superior, inferior, medial, lateral, anterior, posterior, deep
- For positive: extent of involvement

### 7. Lymphovascular Invasion (LVI)
- Not identified
- Present
- Cannot be determined
- Location (intratumoral vs peritumoral) - ICCR non-core

### 8. Regional Lymph Nodes (if submitted)
- Number examined
- Number positive
- Size of largest metastasis
- Presence of extranodal extension
- Method of detection (H&E, IHC)
- Isolated tumor cells (ITCs) vs micrometastases vs macrometastases

### 9. Pathologic Stage (pTNM)
AJCC 8th Edition required:
- pT category (pT1, pT2, pT3, pT4)
- pN category (pN0, pN1, pN2, pN3)
- pM category (if applicable)

### 10. Estrogen Receptor (ER) Status
- Positive (≥1% nuclear staining)
- Low positive (1-10%) - requires notation per ASCO/CAP
- Negative (<1%)
- Percentage of positive cells
- Intensity (weak, moderate, strong)
- Allred score (optional)

### 11. Progesterone Receptor (PR) Status
- Positive (≥1% nuclear staining)
- Negative (<1%)
- Percentage of positive cells
- Intensity

### 12. HER2 Status
- Negative (0 or 1+)
- Equivocal (2+) - requires ISH testing
- Positive (3+)
- ISH results if performed (amplified/not amplified, ratio, copy number)

---

## CONDITIONAL ELEMENTS

### Associated DCIS (if present)
- Extent/size of DCIS
- Nuclear grade (low, intermediate, high)
- Architectural pattern
- Necrosis (comedo vs focal)
- Margin status for DCIS

### Skin Involvement (if skin in specimen)
- Not involved
- Dermal invasion without ulceration
- Ulceration
- Satellite skin nodules
- Inflammatory carcinoma features
- Paget disease

### Skeletal Muscle/Chest Wall (if in specimen)
- Not involved
- Involved

### Nipple (if in specimen)
- Not involved
- Involved by invasive carcinoma
- Paget disease present

### Treatment Effect (post-neoadjuvant)
- No prior treatment / Not applicable
- Residual Cancer Burden (RCB) score
- Response assessment

---

## RECOMMENDED/NON-CORE ELEMENTS

### Tumor Focality
- Unifocal
- Multifocal (specify number, sizes, distances)
- Multicentric

### Perineural Invasion
- Not identified
- Present

### Dermal Lymphatic Invasion
- Not identified
- Present

### Microcalcifications
- Present in invasive carcinoma
- Present in DCIS
- Not identified

### Extensive Intraductal Component (EIC)
- Present (DCIS ≥25% of tumor AND extends beyond invasive edge)
- Not present

### Ki-67 Proliferation Index (ICCR non-core)
- Percentage
- Method of assessment

### Additional Biomarkers
- Oncotype DX / other genomic assays (if performed)

## COMMON SYNONYMS TO RECOGNIZE

- "IDC" = Invasive ductal carcinoma = Invasive carcinoma NST
- "ILC" = Invasive lobular carcinoma
- "LVI" = Lymphovascular invasion
- "PNI" = Perineural invasion
- "SLN" = Sentinel lymph node
- "ALND" = Axillary lymph node dissection
- "EIC" = Extensive intraductal component
- "NST" = No special type
- "NOS" = Not otherwise specified
